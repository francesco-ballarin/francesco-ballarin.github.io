Highlights

New preprint on mathematical analysis and numerical simulations of elastic membranes with deformable boundaries, by F. Ballarin, G. Bevilacqua, L. Lussardi, and A. Marzocchi (July 2022).

I visited the group of Prof. Traian Iliescu at the Interdisciplinary Center for Applied Mathematics, Virginia Tech for 1 month. Thanks to the ARIA MSCA-RISE-2019 project for the financial support (June/July 2022).

New preprint on the application of HiMod to an advection-diffusion problem in microfluidics, by S. Perotto, G. Bellini, F. Ballarin, K. Calò, V. Mazzi, and U. Morbiducci (May 2022).

New preprint on data-driven model reduction using Barlow Twins self-supervised learning, by T. Kadeethum, F. Ballarin, D. O’Malley, Y. Choi, N. Bouklas, and H. Yoon (February 2022).

New preprint on semi-implicit partitioned reduced basis methods for fluid-structure interaction problems, by M. Nonino, F. Ballarin, G. Rozza and Y. Maday (January 2022).

Introducing FEM on Colab, a collection of packages that allows to easily install several finite element libraries on Google Colab. Available packages are FEniCS, FEniCSx, firedrake, gmsh and ngsolve. First released in June 2021, and from January 2022 with periodic builds.

From multiphenics to multiphenicsx, the next generation of easy prototyping of multiphysics problems in FEniCSx - version 0.2.0 released in January 2022. multiphenics is still supported, but new and existing users of FEniCS and multiphenics are encouraged to gradually move to FEniCSx and multiphenicsx.

New preprint on Evolve-Filter-Relax Regularization for reduced order modelling of convection-dominated, marginally-resolved flows, by M. Strazzullo, M. Girfoglio, F. Ballarin, T. Iliescu and G. Rozza (October 2021).

FEMlium - interactive visualization of finite element simulations on geographic maps with folium - development of a new open source library started in May 2021 at Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore.

Bachelor and Master thesis projects available at unicatt under my supervision. Contact me if you are interested!

RBniCS - reduced order modelling in FEniCS - visit the new website www.rbnicsproject.org launched in August 2020
